A cue stores every object's position, gain, mute/solo and effect. Click a cue to recall it; with Morph > 0 the objects glide there over that time. U updates a cue to the current scene. Cues are saved in this browser; Export/Import to move them between machines.

Room & speakers i▾

The area this speaker actually feeds (0 = covers everywhere). Ceiling/Sub: an ellipse footprint — Width/Depth are the half‑sizes, Angle rotates it. Wall: a wedge thrown forward — Throw = how far it reaches, Aim = direction, Beam = cone width. Objects outside it get little feed, so the pan follows the real rig. Panner law i▾

DBAP (distance-based amplitude panning): every object feeds every speaker, weighted by distance, constant-power normalised. Focus tightens each object onto its nearest speakers; Spread widens it across more. The latch lines (links + thicknesses) follow both.
